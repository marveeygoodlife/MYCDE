Top 10 facts about Great-Dane

The Great Dane is an extra-large working dog breed that originated in Germany. They are loyal, intelligent, and trainable. Below are some fascinating facts about this gentle dog breeds;

Gentle Giants:
Great-danes are one of the world tallest dog breeds, often they're referred to as Gentle Giants due to their temperanment.
German Origin:
Despite their name, Great Danes originated in Germany, where they were bred to hunt wild boars and serve as guard dogs.
Tallest Dog Breed:
Great Danes hold the record for the tallest dogs, with some standing over 3 feet (90 cm) at the shoulder. The tallest Great Dane on record, Zeus, measured 44 inches (1.12 meters) at the shoulder.
Short Lifespan:
Unfortunately, due to their large size, Great Danes have a shorter lifespan than smaller breeds, typically living between 7 and 10 years.
Friendly Disposition:
Known for being friendly and affectionate, Great Danes are excellent family pets and often form close bonds with their human companions.
Exercise Needs:
Despite their large size, Great Danes are relatively low-energy dogs that require moderate daily exercise to stay healthy.
Health Issues:
Great Danes are prone to certain health issues, including hip dysplasia, bloat (gastric torsion), and heart conditions like dilated cardiomyopathy.
Coat and Colors:
They have short, smooth coats that come in a variety of colors, including fawn, brindle, blue, black, harlequin, and mantle.
